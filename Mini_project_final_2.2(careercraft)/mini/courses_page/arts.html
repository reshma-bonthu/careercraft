Art Courses: Online, Certifications, Fees, Eligibility, Colleges, Scope 2024

Art courses comprise a mix of theoretical and practical lessons, with a focus on developing skills in various artistic techniques. Students can learn how to create art using traditional methods like paint, pencils, or charcoal, as well as modern-age digital tools like graphic design and animation software.

Some popular art courses include Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA), Bachelor of Visual Arts (BVA), Bachelor of Design (BDes), and Bachelor of Animation. These courses can lead to a wide range of career opportunities and well-paying jobs, such as Graphic Design, Animation, illustration, Game Design, art direction, and more. The average salary offered for such roles ranges between INR 2 – 8 lakhs per annum.

The eligibility criteria to pursue an art course after 12th can vary depending on the specific college and course level. However, the general eligibility criteria is that the candidate must pass the 10+2 exam or equivalent from a recognized board in any stream. The minimum percentage of marks can vary depending on the college, but candidates should aim for 50% or higher. Some colleges may require candidates to take an entrance exam like CUET, NATA, AIEED, or similar.

Diploma and PG Diploma in Art Courses

A diploma or PG Diploma in Art courses is yet another good choice of study in terms of time effectiveness. .

  • Admission to art certificate courses does not require any entrance examinationsDiploma or UG Diploma Art courses can be pursued directly after the 10th or 12th from any stream (Arts, Science, or Commerce) and the admissions in these courses are done based on merit scores in the previous qualification..
  • While a Postgraduate Diploma (PG Diploma) Art course can be done after completion of a Diploma or Bachelor's in a relevant discipline.
  • The top PG Diploma in Arts Colleges like NOU Patna, AMU Aligarh, etc. conduct admissions based on merit or entrance for these courses.
  • A UG Diploma in Arts takes a time span of 2 years to complete whereas a PG Diploma in Arts takes 1 year to complete.
  • Diploma in Arts fees can range around INR 3,000 to NR 1,50,000 and a PG Diploma in Arts can cost around INR 12,000 to INR 4,35,000.
  • Students who have a keen interest in fields like Dance, Creative Art, Photography, Music, etc. can pursue Diploma or PG Diploma in Art courses.
  • The average salary after completion of a Diploma or PG Diploma course in the Art field goes around INR 2,00,000 to INR 4,00,000 per annum in India.

BTech FAQs

Q.Can I learn Art Courses online?

Ans. There are plenty of platforms to learn and practice Art courses online for both free and for fees, at certificate, diploma, and PG Diploma levels. Some of the popular platforms for Online courses are Udemy, Coursera, edX, and FutureLearn. These don’t need any entrance examination and are completely based on the merit score of class 10th or class 12th..

Q.Can I pursue Art courses after completing class 10th?

Ans.Yes, there are various certificate courses and diploma courses available in this field and you can pursue them after completing class 10 depending upon their eligibility criteria. Usually, the eligibility criteria to pursue certificate and diploma courses is the completion of a 10+2 level of education..

Q.What is the average salary offered after completing Art courses?

Ans.. The salary earnings will be completely based on the level of the course learned. The average salary provided in India varies from INR 2 to 8 LPA..

Q.Which art course is the best for students?

Ans. Candidates could pursue the following art courses to get into a successful career: Animation, VFX, Graphic Design, Illustration, Printmaking, Photography, Cinematography, Musical theatre, and Film Production.

Q.Do Art Courses have scope in the future?

Ans.Art is one of the fastest growing industries which has the most scope in the future. Hence, it provides a huge range of job opportunities to the students such as Art Studios, Advertising Companies, Publishing Houses, Product Design, Manufacturing Departments, Magazines, Television, Graphic Arts, Theatre Production, and many more. Hence Art has a bright scope now and even in the future..

Q.Does it cost too much to pursue an Art course in India?

Ans.It depends upon the Institute. Certain institutes charge high, and there are few institutes that charge very affordable fees. Usually, the fees for Art courses in India vary between INR 2,000 and INR 3,00,000.

Q.Which is the best career after completing an Art Course?

Ans. There is a wide range of options when it comes to a career after completing the art courses. Some of the top job choices are Animator, VFX Artist, Teacher, Visualizer, Commercial Artist, Art Director, Illustrator, Curator, Fabricator, Actor, Cartoonist, Musician, and Storyboard Artist. The average salary offered to Art candidates ranges from INR 2 lakhs to INR 8 LPA..

Q.What subjects are taught in art courses?

Ans.he syllabus for the course changes for each college. But there are certain subjects that are general for Art course which includes a history of Indian Art, Indian Aesthetics, Portrait Painting, printmaking, pictorial composition, Art Gallery Management, Indian Culture Arts, 3D Designs, Creative planning, Western Aesthetics, Mural Design, Indian Theatre, Dance Aesthetics, Theory of Dance..

Q.What are the skills required to have a good career in Art?

Ans.candidates pursuing art courses should possess excellent creativity and artistic taste, Excellent drawing skills, time management skills, eye for detail, visual imagination, observation, originality, innovations and storytelling skills..
