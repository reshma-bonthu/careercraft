Law Courses: After 12th, After Graduation, Fees, Admission, Exam Dates 2024

Law courses are sought after by candidates aspiring to pursue a career in law. Students from any academic background can pursue law courses after 12th or graduation. Admission to law courses is based on national or state-level entrance exams like CLAT, LSAT, TS-LAWCET, etc.

Students can specialize in law by pursuing an LLB at the bachelor’s level and an LLM at the master's level. They can also pursue integrated law courses like BBA LLB, BSc LLB, or BCom LLB; which include commerce, management, or science as specializations. Post completing a degree in Law, candidates can choose to make a career as a Lawyer, Judge, Legal Journalist etc. The average starting salary for each profession usually ranges between INR 3.50 – 5 LPA.

Law Course Details

  • BBA LLB, BSc LLB, or BCom LLB, followed by an LLM and PhD in Law are popular Law Courses After the 12th. There's also a wide range of law certificate courses available in India.
  • People from different streams and academic backgrounds can pursue law after class 12 or graduation. 3-year LLB can be pursued after class 12th, but 5-year LLB, LLM, BBA LLB, BSc LLB, or BCom LLB are courses in law after graduation; meaning they require candidates to have a bachelor’s degree from a recognized University.
  • CLAT, LSAT, AILET, and AIBE are national-level Law Entrance Exams.
  • National Law School of India University, Bengaluru, NLU New Delhi, Nalsar University of Law, Hyderabad, and West Bengal National University of Juridical Sciences, Kolkata are among the Top Law Colleges in India.
  • Law course fees range from INR 5,000 to INR 5,00,000 yearly.
  • Law being a vast field for pursuing a career offers a good number of specializations like Media Law, International Law, Air, and Space Law, Corporate Law, Cyber Law, etc.
  • A few important subjects covered under the law are Jurisprudence, Family Laws, Professional Ethics, Crime, Constitutional Law, etc.
  • The popular law course specializations are criminal law, cyber law, and corporate law. Government Lawyers, Law Reporters, Notaries, and Legal Advisors are some notable job prospects after law courses.
  • The Salary of a Lawyer is different for different types of lawyers. Some notable types of lawyers such as attorney lawyers or corporate lawyers make INR 4 – 7 lakhs annually as freshers.

    Law Courses Admission 2024

    • UG Law Admissions: For admission to UG law courses in India, candidates must have passed class 12th in any discipline and must have appeared in the national/ state/ institute level law entrance exams. Admission to the UG law courses is usually on the basis of entrance exams such as the CLAT, LSAT, etc.
    • PG Law Admissions: To be eligible for PG law courses in India, candidates must have completed a graduate degree in the field of law and have a valid entrance exam score. PG law admissions are done on the basis of entrance exams such as AILET, CLAT, LSAT, and others.
    • Diploma and Certificate Law Admissions: Admission to diploma and certificate law admissions are on the basis of class 12th marks. The admission criteria may vary from institute to institute for admission to diploma and certificate law courses.

    Law Courses Eligibility

    Law colleges across India have different selection processes and eligibility criteria for admissions at certificate, diploma, UG, and PG levels. However, given below are the basic eligibility criteria that candidates need to fulfill to secure admission to law courses offered at different levels:

    • Eligibility for certificate or diploma Law Courses: Candidates are eligible to secure admission in certificate or diploma law courses if they have cleared senior secondary/Higher Secondary School/Intermediate (10+2) or its equivalent exam from a recognized board with not less than 45% marks in aggregate.
    • Eligibility for UG Law Courses: Candidates are eligible to secure admission in undergraduate law courses if they have cleared Higher Secondary School/Intermediate (10+2) or its equivalent exam from a recognized board with no less than 45% marks in aggregate.
    • Eligibility for LLB After 12th: For admission to LLB (3 years) course, aspirants need to be graduates from a recognized university. Also, for admission to the LLM course candidates need to possess an LLB or equivalent degree from a recognized university.

    Career in Law After 12th

    • The salary of law graduates depends most on the industry. Every corporate sector needs legal services and apart from that, there are separate legal agencies.
    • When we talk about the corporate world, Telecom is the highest-paying sector for law graduates. After this, consulting companies hire law graduates for legal consulting.
    • Plenty of industries are available for law students to work and some of them are Law firms, MNCs, Government Agencies, Judicial Bodies, Banks, Litigation, etc.
    • They can explore the mentioned industries by getting job roles like Law Officer, Advocate, Litigator, Corporate Lawyer, etc.
    • The average salary offered to law students ranges from INR 4 to 6 Lakh and some of the popular recruiters in this field are Shardul Amarchand Mangaldas & Co., Trilegal India, Khaitan & Co., DSK Legal, Anand & Anand, Desai & Diwanji, etc.

    Career in Law After 12th: Jobs

    Numerous job opportunities are available after the successful completion of Computer Application courses. Some of the popularly known job roles are mentioned here along with their skills, average salary, and job description.

    Job Profile Description Average Annual Salary
    Notary A Notary is someone who acts as a witness to legally binding signatures on different documents. Verification of affidavits, contracts, and various other things are done by them. INR 3,00,000 to INR 3,50,000
    Law Reporter A Law Reporter mostly assists in different law-related reporting. A Law Reporter must have excellent fact-checking skills. They may either work as a freelancer or for specific law firms and agencies. The Law Reporter should also have good copy-editing skills. INR 3,50,000 to INR 4,00,000
    Government Lawyer A Government Lawyer takes care of legal issues related to the Government. He/She will manage different legal documents and provide legal advice to the clients. The Government Lawyer will also represent his/her clients in criminal and legal litigations. INR 6,00,000 to INR 8,00,000
    Legal Advisor A Legal Advisor provides legal advice to those who need it. His responsibilities comprise organizing legal activities, providing legal assistance, creating legal documents, and providing advice on legal issues. INR 4,00,000 to INR 8,00,000
    Law Officer A Law Officer is responsible to regulate all the legal aspects of the organization. Their main job is to keep the organization out of legal trouble. INR 6,50,000 to 8,00,000
    Junior Lawyer Junior lawyers are freshers who have just started their careers. They should work under some advocate for experience and gain the required skills. INR 3,00,000 to 5,00,000
    Advocate Advocates are the legal representatives of a company, person, or client and manage and solve their legal activities and problems from top to bottom. INR 7,00,000 to 10,00,000
